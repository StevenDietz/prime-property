01 — Introduction

A Texas recovery partner for the balances that walk out the door

To Sesi Yerubandi and the Prime Property Management team — you run a Texas multifamily portfolio, and the hardest dollars to recover are the ones a skipped or evicted tenant leaves behind: unpaid rent, damage above the deposit, broken-lease penalties and legal fees.

Unpaid rent isn’t a management problem — it’s a cash-flow problem. The mortgage, taxes, insurance and maintenance don’t pause when a tenant stops paying. And once a tenant vacates, your leverage drops: they’ve moved on, the contact info on file is stale, and some have made themselves hard to find on purpose.

Post-move-out recovery takes what an in-house office can’t sustain — daily skip tracing, credit-bureau and Experian RentBureau reporting, and current Texas landlord-tenant knowledge — applied persistently and compliantly across every account.

That’s our lane. Southwest Recovery Services is a Texas-based property-management collection agency, working residential and commercial balances on pure contingency since 2004. You submit accounts through a secure portal that takes your AppFolio ledger export, and we work them. No recovery, no fee.

03 — Your questions, answered

Your four questions — answered directly

Exactly what you asked, in order, plus the details that matter for a multifamily portfolio.

Your questionSouthwest Recovery Services
1. Contingency fee structure for multifamily & eviction collectionsPure contingency — $0 upfront, no retainer, no monthly minimum; you pay a percentage of what we actually recover, and nothing on accounts that don’t collect. For multifamily and eviction placements the rate typically ranges from 20% to 50%, set by three things: your placement frequency (how regularly you place), the volume of the initial placement opportunity, and the balance amount of the accounts placed. We confirm your rate once we see a representative sample of your AppFolio export.
2. Skip tracing skipped or evicted tenantsExperian-powered skip tracing, refreshed daily, is standard on every placement — not an add-on. When the address and phone on file are stale, we locate the tenant’s current contact information across multiple data sources and work the account until we reach the right party. This is the single biggest lever on post-move-out recovery.
3. Credit bureau reporting timelineWe report qualifying accounts to Equifax and Experian — and, uniquely for landlords, to Experian RentBureau, the rental-payment database that feeds the tenant-screening reports other operators pull. Reporting follows the FDCPA sequence: after placement we send the validation notice, honor the consumer’s 30-day validation/dispute window, and report verified, unresolved accounts after it closes. The collections tradeline can remain up to seven years — and the RentBureau entry follows the tenant to their next lease application. It’s a strong, compliant motivator, and we include RentBureau reporting at no additional charge (see the RentBureau Advantage paper).
4. AppFolio ledger integrationOur secure client portal accepts your AppFolio ledger exports (CSV / Excel) through encrypted file exchange and bulk online placement. We map your export columns — tenant, unit, move-out date, rent, damages, fees — to our intake, so there is no manual re-keying and you can place an entire delinquency batch at once. Direct payments a tenant makes to you are logged back so balances stay accurate.
What we recoverUnpaid rent (current or former tenants), eviction balances and holdover fees, broken-lease penalties, property damage above the security deposit, utility chargebacks, HOA fees and assessments, and commercial lease arrears or early-termination balances.
Texas footprint & complianceTwelve offices across seven states, including six in Texas — Addison (HQ), Austin, Dallas, Houston, Midland and San Antonio. Residential tenant balances are consumer debt worked under the FDCPA and Reg F; commercial accounts under applicable state law. We do not collect consumer debt in CA, OR or WA — a non-issue for a Texas portfolio.
Timing & speedOutreach typically begins within 24–48 hours of a documented placement; most accounts see contact within 48–72 hours. Recovery falls as accounts age, so the balances placed at move-out recover meaningfully better than ones that sit for months.
Legal escalationFor broken-lease refusals, tenants who relocated out of state, or large commercial balances, we review legal options with you — small claims, civil court and judgment enforcement — and keep working recovery alongside your counsel.

04 — How rent & eviction recovery works

Submit the account. We work it.

A clear, six-step process from placement to resolution — the same whether it’s a single skipped tenant or a full delinquency batch out of AppFolio.

1 · Account placement

Provide the tenant name, last known address, balance owed, move-out date and any contact info — or bulk-upload your AppFolio export. Outreach begins within 24–48 hours.

2 · Skip tracing

Experian-powered skip tracing, updated daily, locates current address and phone when the records on file are stale.

3 · Direct contact

We reach the former or current tenant by phone, mail and other permissible channels — professional, FDCPA- and Reg F-compliant communication.

4 · Payment options

Tenants who can’t pay in full get a structured plan. More accounts resolve when there’s a realistic path to zero.

5 · Credit & RentBureau reporting

Qualifying, verified accounts are reported to Equifax, Experian and Experian RentBureau after the validation window — leverage that follows the tenant to their next lease and can remain up to seven years.

6 · Legal escalation

For accounts that don’t resolve through outreach, we review small-claims, civil-court and judgment-enforcement options with you.

No-charge value-add

We report to Experian RentBureau — the database landlords screen against

This is the difference-maker. Beyond reporting to Equifax and Experian, we furnish your bad-debt tenants to Experian RentBureau — the largest rental-payment database (9M+ residents; 3,000+ communities reporting every 24 hours), which feeds the resident-screening reports other operators pull. A skipped tenant’s balance follows them to their next application. And because only FCRA-approved furnishers can report to it, most landlords can’t — we do it for you, at no additional charge.

Leverage where it lands

Skip tracing to find them, plus a RentBureau entry the next landlord sees at screening — the combination that actually moves post-move-out balances.

The Texas clock

Texas sets a four-year statute of limitations on this debt, and it can’t be revived by a payment. The window is finite — place accounts at move-out, not months later.

All reporting is accurate and only after the FDCPA validation window — leverage that protects Prime from liability, not the kind that creates it. Full detail in the RentBureau Advantage white paper.
